## What this page covers

India has a growing ecosystem of online platforms that publish river-related data — from national water quality dashboards to basin-level GIS portals. Most of them are free, publicly accessible, and underused simply because people do not know they exist or how to navigate them.

This page will cover:

- **India-WRIS (Water Resources Information System)**: the national single-window WebGIS portal — what layers it contains, how to find your basin or river stretch, and how to export data
- **CPCB Water Quality Dashboard**: how to find published water quality monitoring data, understand the monitoring station network, and read trend reports
- **NMCG Ganga Dashboard**: the public-facing monitoring and project status interface for the Namami Gange programme
- **State-level portals**: a curated list of state PCB and water department portals with notes on what each publishes and how reliably it is updated
- **Satellite-based platforms**: global tools like Google Earth Engine, Copernicus Open Access Hub, and NASA Earthdata that can be used to observe river extent, turbidity, and land-use change over time

<Aside type="caution" title="Data freshness warning">
Many official Indian water portals suffer from irregular updates and inconsistent coverage. This page will clearly flag which portals have reliable, near-real-time data and which should be treated as historical or indicative only.
</Aside>

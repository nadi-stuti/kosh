## Why read before you go?

You do not need to understand river science deeply before your first cleanup. But a few well-chosen articles will help you observe more, understand what you are seeing, and contribute more meaningfully from day one.

This reading path takes approximately **45–60 minutes** and covers the minimum useful foundation for a first-time volunteer.

---

## The reading path (coming soon)

The final path will link to 4–5 articles from [Pollution Academy](/pollution-academy/) covering:

- **What a river basin is and why it matters** — from the *Big Picture* module of [Why Rivers Are Polluted](/pollution-academy/why-rivers-are-polluted/01-big-picture/).
- **The main visible sources of river pollution** — from [Main Sources](/pollution-academy/why-rivers-are-polluted/02-main-sources/); helps you identify what you see on the ground.
- **What the basic water quality indicators mean** — from [How Pollution Is Measured](/pollution-academy/how-pollution-is-measured/water-quality-parameters/); so test results and visual surveys make sense.
- **Who is working on rivers in India** — from [Who Is Doing What](/pollution-academy/who-is-doing-what/); helps you understand the ecosystem you are joining.

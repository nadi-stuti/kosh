## What this guide will cover

A step-by-step action guide for filing a formal pollution complaint in India — covering online portals, offline procedures, and what to do if your complaint goes unanswered.

**Planned content:**

<Steps>
1. **Document the incident first** — what you need before filing: photos, GPS location, date/time, description of the source. Link to [Visual River Survey Basics](/get-involved/ground-work/beginner/visual-river-survey-basics/) and [Documenting & Reporting Findings](/get-involved/ground-work/advanced/documenting-and-reporting-findings/).

2. **Choose the right authority** — when to go to CPCB vs your State Pollution Control Board vs local municipal body vs district collector. Decision tree based on type and scale of incident.

3. **File online via CPCB's complaint portal** — step-by-step walkthrough of the CPCB Grievance portal: account creation, complaint form, attaching evidence, and submission.

4. **File via State PCB** — how State PCB complaint processes differ; links to state-wise portal directory (coming soon).

5. **File offline** — how to submit a written complaint at a district or sub-divisional office; what the letter must contain; sample format.

6. **Track your complaint** — how to follow up, what timelines to expect, and what escalation looks like if there is no response within the standard window.

7. **Escalate if ignored** — RTI application basics, approaching the NGT (National Green Tribunal), and when to involve a lawyer or NGO.
</Steps>

<Aside type="note" title="Using CPCB data in your complaint">
Official CPCB monitoring data strengthens a complaint significantly. See the [CPCB Dashboard Guide](/get-involved/digital-tools/cpcb-dashboard-guide/) for how to retrieve and reference it.
</Aside>

## What this guide will cover

A practical, no-equipment guide to observing and recording the visible condition of a river stretch.

**Planned content:**

- **What a visual survey is** — its purpose, its limits, and when it is enough vs when you need to escalate to water sampling.
- **What to look for** — colour and clarity of water, foam and surface films, visible waste types, bank conditions, odour, and signs of dead aquatic life.
- **How to record your observations** — a standardised observation sheet template (printable and digital) compatible with Nadikosh's reporting format.
- **Photo documentation** — how to photograph effectively for records (angles, lighting, GPS tagging on your phone).
- **How to interpret what you see** — a simple guide to common visible signs and what they suggest. Links to relevant [Pollution Library](/pollution-library/) articles for deeper explanation.
- **What to do with your data** — how to submit your observation to Nadikosh or your NGO partner.

# 🏕️ Ground Work

This is the core field action section of Nadikosh.

Every guide here is a **protocol or playbook** — a clear goal, a checklist, step-by-step instructions, and a defined outcome. Nothing here explains *why* rivers are polluted. If you need that foundation first, visit [Pollution Library](/pollution-academy/).

<Aside type="tip" title="Check your skill level first">
Guides in this section are tagged Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced based on real-world field experience — not academic knowledge. If you haven't taken the [Skill Assessment Quiz](/get-involved/#skill-quiz) yet, do it now. Articles will warn you if a guide is above your current assessed level.
</Aside>

---

## Skill levels at a glance

| Level | Who it's for | Typical activities |
|---|---|---|
| <Badge text="Beginner" variant="success" /> | First-timers, students, one-time volunteers | Cleanup participation, visual surveys, basic gear |
| <Badge text="Intermediate" variant="tip" /> | Regular volunteers, NGO field staff | Audits, basic sampling, group coordination |
| <Badge text="Advanced" variant="danger" /> | Experienced field officers, programme leads | Citizen science, long-term monitoring, formal documentation |

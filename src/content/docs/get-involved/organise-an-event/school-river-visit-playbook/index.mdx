## What this playbook will cover

A complete guide for educators, NGO staff, or volunteers organising a structured river site visit for a school group in India.

**Planned content:**

- **Age-appropriate planning** — how the visit structure changes for primary, middle, and high school groups; learning objectives for each.
- **School coordination checklist** — what to confirm with the school administration, parent permissions, and teacher briefing.
- **Site assessment** — how to evaluate a river site for a school visit (safety, accessibility, visual impact, toilet facilities).
- **Activity structure** — a sample 2-hour visit schedule with activities: visual survey introduction, waste identification exercise, Q&A, reflection.
- **Safety protocol for minors** — additional safety rules, supervision ratios, and emergency contacts specific to school groups.
- **Connecting to curriculum** — how to frame the visit in terms of NCERT environmental science and geography topics for student relevance.
- **Follow-up in the classroom** — suggested post-visit activities that teachers can use to extend the learning; links to relevant [Pollution Academy](/pollution-academy/) articles suitable for students.

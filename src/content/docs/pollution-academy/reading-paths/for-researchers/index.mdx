## Your goal

You are not starting from zero. You have a working knowledge of environmental issues and want to use Pollution Library to **orient yourself in the Indian river ecosystem** — understand the institutional architecture, evaluate the data landscape, and find reliable entry points for deeper investigation.

This path is deliberately shorter and more selective. It skips introductory content and focuses on what is most useful for someone working at a professional or research level.

---

## Your reading sequence

<Steps>

1. **Understand the monitoring infrastructure and its gaps**

   [Monitoring Methods](/pollution-academy/how-pollution-is-measured/02-monitoring-methods/)

   Before trusting any Indian river data, understand how it was collected, by whom, and where the coverage fails.

2. **Develop critical data literacy**

   [Reading Data and Reports](/pollution-academy/how-pollution-is-measured/03-reading-data-and-reports/)

   How to interrogate a WQI score, what CPCB reporting conventions mean, and where methodological weaknesses commonly appear.

3. **Map the governance architecture**

   [Government Agencies](/pollution-academy/who-is-doing-what/government-agencies/)

   The statutory framework — who has mandate over what — is essential context for any policy or applied research.

4. **Survey the research institution landscape**

   [Research Institutions](/pollution-academy/who-is-doing-what/research-institutions/)

   Key academic groups producing peer-reviewed river science in India, and where their outputs are accessible.

5. **Evaluate citizen science as a data source**

   [Citizen Science Apps](/pollution-academy/tools-and-tech/citizen-science-apps/)

   Protocols, validation standards, and an honest assessment of where community-generated data is and is not research-grade.

6. **Engage with the international data layer**

   [International Bodies](/pollution-academy/who-is-doing-what/international-bodies/)

   Global datasets, UN monitoring frameworks, and international NGO programmes that intersect with Indian river research.

7. **Read a stretch case study as a methods example**

   [Stretch Case Studies](/pollution-academy/stretch-case-studies/)

   Each case study documents data sources, methods used, and limitations — useful as a model for your own work.

</Steps>

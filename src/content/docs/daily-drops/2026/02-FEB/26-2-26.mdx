Unlike traditional dams that rely mainly on gravity, Kaleshwaram is built around a different idea — lifting water. Massive pump houses draw water from the Godavari and raise it in stages across elevations, allowing it to reach higher and drier regions. It is one of the largest multi-stage lift irrigation projects in the world.

The project consists of a network of barrages, reservoirs, tunnels, canals, and underground pump houses. The scale of the machinery involved is enormous — giant pumps capable of moving thousands of cubic feet of water per second.

Water is lifted in multiple stages, in some places by over 100 meters, through a carefully designed cascade of reservoirs. The engineering required precise synchronization of pumping systems, tunnel construction through rock, and integration of large-scale power supply to operate the lifting mechanism.
